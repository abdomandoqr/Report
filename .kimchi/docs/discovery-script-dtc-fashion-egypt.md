# Discovery Call Script: DTC Fashion Store Owner in Egypt

**For:** Database Reactivation Workflow (Customer Winback / Retention)
**Target:** DTC Fashion & Apparel store owner, 2+ years, 2,000+ past buyers, Excel/Google Sheets ops
**Call duration:** 15–20 minutes
**Your goal:** Get them to admit how much dormant customer revenue they're leaving on the table, and quantify it in exact EGP.

---

## 1. The Call Frame (Verbatim Opener)

> *"Salaam [Name], thanks for making the time — I know how packed your day gets with orders and follow-ups and logistics. Here's how I want to use the next 15 minutes: I just want to ask you a few questions about your store, how you find customers, and what happens after they buy once. Then, if it sounds like what I do could help, I'll tell you exactly what that is. If it doesn't, no problem — we'll end the call and you won't hear from me again. Sound good?"*

**Pause. Wait for them to say yes. Do not skip the pause.**

> *"Great. So I work with a few fashion brands in Cairo and Alexandria — mostly helping them with the backend side: getting past customers to come back instead of constantly spending on Meta to find strangers. But every store is different, so before I assume anything, tell me about yours."*

---

## 2. Rapport Questions (2–3 minutes)

**Q1: "How long have you been running [Brand], and what made you start it?"**

*What to listen for:*
- **Green flag:** "3 years now" = ICP match. "I started from my apartment" = founder-operator, no big team.
- **Yellow flag:** "Just launched last month" = not ICP yet. Ask: "How many orders so far?" If under 500, pivot politely.
- **Red flag:** "I also import electronics / also run a dropshipping Telegram channel" = not a focused DTC brand.

**Q2: "What do you actually sell day to day — is it your own designs, local manufacturing, imported, or a mix?"**

*What to listen for:*
- **Green flag:** "Local production" or "we manufacture in Obour / 10th of Ramadan" = understands margins + repeat-purchase potential.
- **Green flag:** "Apparel, some accessories" = high-frequency, repeat-purchase = perfect for reactivation.
- **Red flag:** "We sell everything — electronics, kitchen stuff, fashion sometimes" = general store, no brand, no customer loyalty.

**Q3: "What's the one thing you're proudest of about the brand right now?"**

*What to listen for:*
- Builds emotional investment. Gets them talking positively before you ask about pain.
- Note the word — "proud of our Instagram community" = they care about engagement, not just transactions.
- Note if they say nothing — if they can't name something they're proud of, they're burned out. Burned-out founders are desperate for solutions that reduce their workload.

---

## 3. Context Questions (2–3 minutes)

**Q4: "Walk me through a normal day — how do orders come in, and what do you spend most of your time on?"**

*What to listen for:*
- **Green flag:** "WhatsApp all day, then pack, then reconciliation with Bosta" = ops-heavy, no time for marketing, they need automation.
- **Green flag:** "Meta Ads in the morning, then order confirmations, then follow-ups" = spending money on acquisition but not retention.
- **Red flag:** "I have a team of 10 handling everything" = too big, not the solo/founder-operator ICP. Could be good target but different sales cycle.

**Q5: "How are you tracking orders and customers right now — Shopify dashboard, Excel sheets, something else?"**

*What to listen for:*
- **Green flag (this is everything):** "Excel" or "Google Sheets" or "I export from Shopify to a CSV once a month" = they have data but are doing NOTHING with it. Perfect fit.
- **Green flag:** "Shopify admin, but I never look at customer reports" = same thing — data exists, no action.
- **Yellow flag:** "We use a CRM" = might have some retention. Probe: "Do you regularly message past customers?"
- **Red flag:** "I don't really track it" = too early or too disorganized. Not your customer yet.

**Q6: "What does a typical month look like in terms of orders — rough numbers?"**

*What to listen for:*
- **Green flag:** "About 1,500–2,000 orders a month" = healthy enough to have a real database.
- **Green flag:** "We did 3,000 last Ramadan, maybe 800 off-season" = seasonal spikes with dormant periods = reactivation gold.
- **Yellow flag:** "Maybe 100–200 orders a month" = not enough history. Say: "What stage are you in?" If early-stage, maybe nurture.
- **Red flag:** "I don't really know, our accountant handles that" = not operator-close enough to the business.

---

## 4. Problem-Finding Questions (5–8 minutes)

**Q7 (The Funnel Opener): "Where do most of your customers come from right now — is it Meta, TikTok, word of mouth, or something else?"**

*What to listen for:*
- They'll say Meta/Facebook 90% of the time.
- **Dig deeper:** "And how much are you spending on Meta per month, roughly?"
- Write down the number. Do not skip this.
- Then ask: "What's your ROAS usually look like?" You already know the answer is likely 2.5×–4× and dropping.

**Q8: "How much of your monthly revenue would you say comes from new people finding you for the first time vs. people who already bought before?"**

*What to listen for:*
- **Green flag (the admission):** "Probably 80–90% new" or "Mostly new customers" or "I don't really get many repeat buyers."
- When they say this, you are 80% to a close. This is the exact problem.
- **Yellow flag:** "Maybe 60–70% new" = some repeat, but still heavy on acquisition.
- **Follow-up:** "Why do you think people don't come back? Is it the product, the experience, or you just don't reach them again?"
- Let them talk. Do not interrupt. They will diagnose their own problem.

**Q9: "When someone buys from you once, what happens next? Do you have a system to get them to buy again, or is it just hoping they remember your Instagram?"**

*What to listen for:*
- **Green flag (the pain):** "Nothing — we just post and hope" or "I try to DM them but I get busy" or "I don't have time to follow up."
- **Green flag:** "We have an email list but we never email them" = asset sitting dormant = your exact pitch.
- **Yellow flag:** "We send Eid discount emails sometimes" = inconsistent, no system.
- **Red flag:** "We have a loyalty program" = might already have retention. Probe: "How many active members? What % of revenue comes from it?"

**Q10: "You mentioned you have [X] orders a month — do you know what percentage of those come from brand-new customers who never bought before?"**

*What to listen for:*
- They probably don't know. That's okay.
- Say: "No problem, most founders don't. But if you had to guess — of your last 100 orders, how many were first-time buyers?"
- If they guess 70+ = acquisition addiction. This is where you say: *"So you're spending 2,000–3,000 EGP a day to bring strangers to your store, while the 4,000 people who already bought from you are basically hearing nothing from you. Does that sound about right?"*
- Let them agree. This is the moment they sell themselves.

**Q11: "Have you ever calculated how many past customers you have who bought once and never came back?"**

*What to listen for:*
- **Green flag:** "No, but it must be thousands" or "I have a spreadsheet with 5,000 names" = perfect. The asset exists.
- **Green flag:** "I know it's a lot" = they feel the weight.
- **Dig deeper:** "If you had to guess — out of all the people who ever bought from you, what % bought a second time?"
- Industry average in Egypt fashion DTC: 15–25%. If they say anything under 30%, there's a massive opportunity.
- **The pivot:** *"So if 80% of your past buyers only bought once, and each of those orders was worth what — 400 EGP? Then you're sitting on a pretty big asset that nobody's touching."*

---

## 5. Dollar-Quantifying Questions (3–5 minutes)

**Q12: "Let's put some numbers on this. What does it cost you to get one new customer through Meta right now — roughly?"**

*What to listen for:*
- If they know: write it down. Typical answer: 200–400 EGP per new customer.
- If they don't know, help them calculate: *"So you said Meta is [X] EGP a month and you get [Y] orders. Quick division — that's about [Z] EGP per order. And if [80%] of those are new customers, you're paying about [Z × 0.8] to acquire one new person. Sound accurate?"*
- Get that number. Do not move on without it.

**Q13: "And what does a typical customer spend with you in that first order?"**

*What to listen for:*
- Average answer: 250–800 EGP.
- Now calculate silently: if CAC is 300 EGP and AOV is 500 EGP, they might be breaking even or losing money on first order.
- **Do not say this yet.** File it for the close call.

**Q14: "If you had a way to reach out to your past buyers — the ones who already bought once or twice — and get even 10% of them to come back and buy again, what would that be worth to you? Let's do the math together."**

*Make them calculate out loud:*

> *"So you said you have [X] total past buyers. If 80% bought once and never came back, that's [X × 0.8] dormant customers. If we could get 10% of those to buy again, that's [X × 0.8 × 0.1] orders. At [Y] EGP average order, that's [X × 0.8 × 0.1 × Y] = [Z] EGP. Does that sound roughly right?"*

*What to listen for:*
- They will do the math and the number will shock them. Often 20,000–100,000+ EGP.
- **Green flag:** "Wow, I never thought about it that way" = they see the opportunity.
- **Green flag:** "That would basically pay for my next collection" = they have a specific use case for the revenue.
- If the number is small (under 10,000 EGP), they might not be at ICP scale yet.

**Q15: "Here's what I'm curious about: if you already HAVE the revenue sitting there, why do you think you haven't extracted it? What's stopping you from personally reaching out to those past customers?"**

*What to listen for:*
- **Green flag:** "No time" or "Too many customers" or "I don't know what to say" or "WhatsApp is already exploding" = operational pain, not strategic rejection.
- **Green flag:** "I thought about it but I wouldn't know what offer to make" = awareness + uncertainty.
- **Yellow flag:** "I don't think they'd come back" = self-limiting belief. Reframe: "Why not? They already trusted you enough to buy once."
- **Red flag:** "I'm planning to build that myself" or "My cousin is making an app for that" = DIY objection. File for close call.

## 6. The Soft Close (2–3 minutes)

**Recap (show them you listened):**

> *"So let me make sure I've got this right. You're running [Brand] for [X] years now. You're doing about [Y] orders a month, and [~80%] are brand-new customers from Meta. You're spending [Z] EGP a day on Meta, which is about [A] EGP per new customer. You have [B] past buyers sitting in [Excel/Shopify/CSV], and most of them bought once and never came back. If even 10% of those dormant customers bought again, that could be worth roughly [C] EGP. And right now, there's basically no system to reach them — it would take too much time, and you don't know what to say anyway. Is that accurate?"*

**Wait. Let them confirm. Nod.**

**Book the close call:**

> *"Here's what I'd like to do. Let me put together a specific reactivation campaign plan for [Brand] — using your actual numbers, your customer list, and your brand voice. It'll be a one-page proposal showing exactly what we'd send, who we'd send it to, and what I'd expect to recover. I'll have it ready in 24 hours. Can we get back on a call [tomorrow / day after] so I can walk you through it?"*

**If they hesitate:**

> *"No commitment on this next call — it's just me showing you what it could look like. Then you decide if it makes sense."*

**If they say yes:**

> *"Perfect. I'll need one thing from you before the next call: your customer list in a CSV or Excel. Just customer names, emails, and last purchase dates. So I can show you exactly how many dormant customers you have and what they're worth. Can you get that to me today?"*

**If they say no to the data sharing:**

> *"No problem — I can build the plan with estimates based on what you told me. But the real power is when you see the actual number of dormant customers in black and white. Up to you."*

## 8. Common Objections to Watch For

### Objection 1: "We already send manual WhatsApp messages sometimes."

**The phrase:** *"I already message past customers on WhatsApp."*

**The reframe (no pitch):** *"That's great — it means you already know the channel works. How many customers did you message last month, and how long did it take you?"*

**File for close call:** Calculate their manual effort vs. automation value. If they messaged 50 customers in 3 hours, they understand the concept but are bottlenecked by time. The close call pitch: "What if that same 3 hours messaged 500 customers with personalized AI offers?"

---

### Objection 2: "Our customers don't really buy again — fashion is one-time."

**The phrase:** *"People buy a dress and they don't need another one."*

**The reframe (no pitch):** *"That's fair — some product categories are one-and-done. But do they ever buy accessories, a different size, a gift, or seasonal pieces? And what about the ones who bought a basic — like a black top or linen set — wouldn't they need a different color or a replacement in 6 months?"*

**File for close call:** Show category-based reactivation (Eid collection, summer drop, gifting occasions). Also: even if true, 10% reactivation rate × thousands of one-time buyers = still massive.

---

### Objection 3: "We're too busy right now / maybe later."

**The phrase:** *"We're in the middle of a new collection launch / Ramadan season / etc."*

**The reframe (no pitch):** *"I get it — collections and seasons are everything. That's actually exactly why this makes sense right now. Most of your past customers bought your LAST collection. If you could reach them before the launch instead of just hoping they see your Instagram, the new collection would launch to a warm audience, not just strangers from Meta."*

**File for close call:** Tie reactivation to their next seasonal drop. The campaign CAN'T wait — it needs to run *before* the drop, not after.

---

### Objection 4: "We don't have a big enough customer list."

**The phrase:** *"We only have a few hundred customers."*

**The reframe (no pitch):** *"That's good information — how many exactly? If it's under 2,000 past buyers, you might be right that reactivation won't move the needle yet. The math really starts working around 2,000+. How fast are you adding new customers?"*

**File for close call:** If they're at 1,500 and growing fast, give them a timeline. If they're at 300, politely disqualify and ask them to reach out when they hit 1,500.

---

### Objection 5: "How is this different from email marketing?"

**The phrase:** *"We tried Mailchimp / Klaviyo and it didn't work."*

**The reframe (no pitch):** *"What happened when you tried it? Was it the open rates, the content, or did you just not have time to manage it?"*

**File for close call:** Most Egyptian DTC brands "tried" email but sent 2 blast campaigns a year in English. They never did: AI-personalized Arabic messages, tiered offers by churn score, WhatsApp/Instagram DM channels, or A/B testing. The close call differentiator: *"This isn't email marketing. It's a reactivation system built specifically for Egyptian COD-heavy DTC — with AI offers and DM delivery."*

---

### Objection 6: "Can I see results from another fashion brand first?"

**The phrase:** *"Do you have a case study from a similar brand?"*

**The reframe (no pitch):** *"Totally fair. Let me ask it differently: if I can show you exactly what your dormant customers are worth using your actual data — not someone else's — and then run a pilot campaign on a small group for free so you see the results yourself, would that get you comfortable?"*

**File for close call:** The "free reactivation audit" + small pilot group (50–100 customers) is the killer close. Results from THEIR customers > any case study.

## 9. Post-Call Checklist (Do Within 10 Minutes)

Immediately after the call ends:

- [ ] **Write down the CAC number** they quoted (EGP per new customer)
- [ ] **Write down the AOV** they quoted
- [ ] **Write down their estimated dormant customer count** and the 10% reactivation value you calculated together
- [ ] **Note their biggest stated pain** (time? creative fatigue? cash flow?)
- [ ] **Note any objection** they raised
- [ ] **Send a WhatsApp message within 1 hour:** *"Salaam [Name], great speaking with you. Attached is a quick summary of the numbers we ran today — [X dormant customers] worth roughly [Y] EGP. I'll have the campaign plan ready for our call on [date]. Talk soon."*
- [ ] **If they agreed to send data:** Follow up within 4 hours if you haven't received it.
