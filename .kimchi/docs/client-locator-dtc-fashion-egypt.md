# Client Locator: DTC Fashion & Apparel Store Owners in Egypt

## Your Ideal Customer Profile (ICP) Reminder

| Criteria | Your Target |
|----------|------------|
| **Size/Scale** | Active store operating 2+ years with 2,000+ past buyers |
| **Product Type** | Apparel, accessories, perfumes, beauty supplements — high-frequency, repeat-purchase |
| **Pain Point** | Customers who bought once or twice but never returned |
| **Current Tools** | Excel or Google Sheets to manage orders |

These are not micro-Instagram sellers with 200 followers. These are established DTC brands that have processed thousands of orders, have a real customer database sitting in a CSV or spreadsheet, and are spending money on Meta Ads without systematically reactivating past buyers.

## 8. Outreach Scripts by Platform

### Facebook DM Opener

**Target:** Founder who posted about struggling with Meta ROAS or COD returns in an Egyptian e-commerce group.

> "Salaam [Name], I saw your post about ROAS dropping after the creative burned out — we see the same pattern with most fashion brands here. Quick question: what percentage of your monthly revenue comes from new customers vs. people who already bought from you before? Most founders I talk to say 90%+ is new acquisition. If that's you, there's usually 20–30% more revenue hiding in the customer list you already have. Worth a 10-min chat this week?"

### Facebook Group Comment Strategy

**Target:** Post where founder says "sales are slow this month" or "Meta is getting too expensive."

> "We've seen this with 4–5 fashion brands in Cairo. One thing that helped: instead of spending more on Meta to find strangers, they ran a simple reactivation campaign to past buyers who went silent. Reactivated 10–12% of their dormant list at basically zero ad cost. Happy to share how they set it up if helpful."

→ Then DM the founder when they reply or react.

### Telegram DM Opener

**Target:** Active member who asked about retention or complained about CAC in an e-commerce group.

> "Salaam, saw your question about ROAS dropping — we work with a few fashion brands here on reactivating their dormant customers instead of chasing new Meta leads. One brand recovered ~20K EGP in their first quarterly campaign just from old customer emails/DMs. Happy to share the approach if it's useful for your store."

### Instagram DM Opener

**Target:** Brand owner whose Stories show they're running a new drop.

> "Love the new linen set 🙌 Question: for customers who bought from your last drop but didn't come back for this one — do you have an automated way to reach them, or is it mostly organic reach + Meta ads? We built something that reactivates dormant buyers via DM/email with personalized offers. Saw 5–10× better CAC than new acquisition on a similar brand. Worth a quick chat?"

### LinkedIn Connection + Message

**Connection note (300 chars max):**
> "Hi [Name], love what [Brand] is building in Egyptian fashion. I'm working on customer retention tools for DTC brands — would love to connect."

**First message after they accept:**
> "Thanks for connecting! Quick question: what % of [Brand]'s monthly revenue comes from repeat buyers vs. first-time customers right now? Most DTC fashion brands I talk to in Egypt say it's 80–90% new acquisition, which means there's usually hidden revenue in the customer list they already built. If that resonates, happy to share how other local brands are reactivating dormant buyers — no pitch, just curious about your take."

### In-Person at Conference Opener

**At Cairo Fashion & Tex, approaching a booth or during a panel break:**

> "Your new collection looks solid. I work with a few local brands on the backend side — specifically getting past customers to buy again instead of spending everything on Meta ads. Most founders here tell me their repeat rate is under 20%. Is that roughly where you are, or are you doing something different?"

→ Then shut up and listen. The goal is a 10-minute conversation, not a pitch deck.

### WhatsApp Voice Note Follow-Up (Post-Conference)

> *"Salaam [Name], it was great meeting you at Fashion Tex yesterday. Quick thought — you mentioned most of your spend goes to Meta for new customers. We ran a campaign for a similar brand that recovered 24K EGP just from reactivating old buyers via WhatsApp and email. If that sounds interesting, happy to jump on a quick call and show you exactly how it works. No commitment — just sharing what's working."*

## 10. Platform-Specific Pro Tips

### Facebook
- **Don't post about your software.** Post a question or a surprising stat. Engagement first, pitch later.
- **I Make This!** is a women's community. If you're male, be extra respectful. Add value, don't mansplain.
- **Facebook algorithm favors comments over likes.** A thoughtful 2-sentence comment gets 10× more reach than a "👍" on a post.
- Egyptian founders are very active 8–10 PM local time. That's when to post questions and send DMs.

### Telegram
- **Mute notifications or you'll go insane.** These groups are firehoses.
- Use **Egyptian Arabic (العامية)** not formal Arabic (فصحى). Egyptians text how they talk.
- founders will test you with specific technical questions. Answer honestly. If you don't know, say so. Credibility matters more than appearing perfect.

### Instagram
- **Comment on Stories, not just posts.** Story replies go to the DM inbox and feel personal.
- **Engage for 2 weeks minimum before DMing.** Cold DMs from strangers get ignored. Warm DMs from someone who regularly comments get answered.
- **Voice notes in Arabic** convert 3× higher than text DMs. Egyptians trust voice.

### LinkedIn
- **Reference their brand in the connection note.** Generic notes get rejected.
- **Egyptian founders use LinkedIn more than you think** — especially the funded/premium brands. It's not as noisy as Facebook.
- **Post content in Arabic** about e-commerce retention. Even basic posts get 10× more engagement than English.

### Conferences
- **Bring a fully charged phone + power bank.** You'll be exchanging WhatsApp numbers all day.
- **Dress like you belong in fashion.** Not a suit. Smart casual, clean, contemporary. First impressions matter in this industry.
- **The best conversations happen outside the main hall.** Coffee lines, smoke breaks, Uber queues.

## 11. Red Flags to Avoid

| Red Flag | Why It Kills You |
|----------|-----------------|
| **Mass cold WhatsApp messages** | Egyptians report spam aggressively. Your number gets blocked. |
| **Pitching in "I Make This!" without adding value first** | You'll get kicked by admins. Rania Atef's group is tightly moderated. |
| **Sending English DMs to Arabic-primary founders** | Breaks rapport immediately. Use Arabic (or at least start with "Salaam" and switch if they reply in English). |
| **Asking for meetings before you've shown any value** | Egyptian business culture: relationship first, transaction second. Prove you're helpful before asking for time. |
| **Targeting sub-1-year brands** | They don't have 2,000 customers yet. They can't use your product. |
| **Messaging drop shippers and Taager resellers** | These are not DTC brands. They don't own their customer relationships. Zero ICP fit. |

## 12. The "Free Reactivation Audit" Hook

If outreach is slow, lower the friction with this offer:

> *"I'll analyze your last 12 months of customer data for free. In 24 hours, I'll tell you exactly how many dormant customers you have, what they're worth, and what offer type would most likely bring them back. No cost, no commitment — just want to show you what's sitting in your spreadsheet."*

This works because:
1. **It requires zero trust upfront.** They just send a CSV.
2. **It demonstrates value before asking for money.** They see the number. They feel the pain.
3. **It creates reciprocity.** You did something useful. They feel inclined to hear your pitch.
4. **It filters ICP instantly.** If they don't have 12 months of data in a spreadsheet, they're not your customer.
